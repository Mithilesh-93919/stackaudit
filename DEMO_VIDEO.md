# DEMO VIDEO — StackAudit

A concise guide for recording and publishing the StackAudit product demo.

---

## Video Metadata

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Loom title** | `StackAudit — AI Spend Audit SaaS Demo` |
| **Duration** | 2–3 minutes |
| **Resolution** | 1280×800 minimum (1920×1080 preferred) |
| **Browser** | Chrome, clean window, no extensions visible |
| **Starting URL** | https://stackaudit-fawn.vercel.app |
| **Theme** | Start in dark mode (default), toggle to light near end |

---

## Pre-Recording Checklist

- [ ] Open Chrome in a clean incognito window (hides bookmarks bar and extensions)
- [ ] Set browser zoom to 100%
- [ ] Clear any existing `stackaudit_wizard_draft` from localStorage (DevTools → Application → Local Storage)
- [ ] Open https://stackaudit-fawn.vercel.app and confirm the page loads
- [ ] Mute your phone and close Slack/notification apps
- [ ] If using Loom: set to "Full Screen" or "Window" capture mode

---

## Full Recording Script

### [0:00–0:20] Opening — Landing Page

**Action:** Open the live URL in a clean window. Let the page load and settle. Slowly scroll down past the hero headline, then scroll back up.

**Narration / talking point:**
> "This is StackAudit — an AI spend audit tool for startup teams. It analyzes what you're paying for tools like ChatGPT, Cursor, and GitHub Copilot, and tells you exactly where you're wasting money. No account required."

**Visual elements to highlight:**
- The headline "Stop Paying for AI Tools Your Team Doesn't Use"
- Tool badge row at the bottom (ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, Cursor, etc.)

---

### [0:20–0:35] Entering the Audit Wizard

**Action:** Click the **"Audit My Stack — Free"** CTA button in the hero.

**Talking point:**
> "Let's run an audit. The wizard has 4 steps and takes under 5 minutes."

**Visual elements:** The smooth page transition into `/audit/new` and the Step 1 form appearing instantly (server pre-rendered, no loading flash).

---

### [0:35–0:50] Step 1 — Team Context

**Action:** Click the **"10"** quick-select button for team size. Optionally type "Acme Startup" in the Company Name field. Click **Continue**.

**Talking point:**
> "Team size is used to detect seat overprovisioning — if you're paying for 10 seats but only 6 people are active, that's flagged."

---

### [0:50–1:10] Step 2 — Select Your AI Stack

**Action:** On the tool selection grid, click **GitHub Copilot** and **Claude** to add them (Cursor and ChatGPT should already be selected). Show the "4 tools selected" counter at the bottom.

**Talking point:**
> "Select all the AI tools your team pays for. The engine knows about 8 tools including API tiers for OpenAI and Anthropic."

---

### [1:10–1:30] Step 3 — Plan Details

**Action:** Keep the default plan settings. Point out the **"Active seats"** field for one tool and show that it's less than purchased seats — the amber "Unused seats" warning appears.

**Talking point:**
> "Each tool gets its own plan tier, seat count, monthly spend, and active seat declaration. The engine uses this to detect idle seats and downgrade opportunities."

---

### [1:30–1:40] Step 4 — Review

**Action:** Continue to the review screen. Show the **"Declared monthly AI spend"** total ($340/month in our example).

**Talking point:**
> "Review your inputs. The declared spend here is what the engine will audit against."

---

### [1:40–1:55] Running the Audit

**Action:** Click **"Run Audit"**. Let the full loading animation play through — it cycles through diagnostic steps ("Analyzing seat capacity...", "Running overlap detection...").

**Talking point:**
> "The engine processes on the server — this takes under a second. The loading animation cycles through diagnostic steps while the request completes."

---

### [1:55–2:15] Results Dashboard

**Action:** Show the full results dashboard. Point out:
1. The **Health Score ring** (21/100 in our example)
2. The big **"Save $270 / month"** headline
3. The **$3,240/year** annualized figure
4. The AI-generated executive summary paragraph
5. The **Current vs. Optimized cost bar**

**Talking point:**
> "The health score is a 0–100 composite — lower means more waste. The savings figure is deterministic math, not AI estimates. The AI summary on top is generated by Claude Haiku from the structured output."

---

### [2:15–2:30] Expanding a Finding

**Action:** Click on the **"Consolidate 2 overlapping coding tools"** card (Cursor + GitHub Copilot finding, $200/month savings). Expand it to show the action checklist inside.

**Talking point:**
> "Every finding has a severity, confidence rating, and a step-by-step action checklist. This one flags Cursor and GitHub Copilot as redundant — pick one."

---

### [2:30–2:45] Shareable Report

**Action:** Click the **"Share Audit Card"** button in the top-right. A toast appears confirming the link was copied.

**Talking point:**
> "Every audit gets a public shareable link. Send it to your CFO, your engineering lead, or your investors. No login required to view it."

---

### [2:45–2:55] Dark / Light Mode

**Action:** Click the theme toggle in the navbar. Select **Light** mode. Show the dashboard in light mode for 3 seconds. Toggle back to dark.

**Talking point:**
> "Full system-aware dark and light mode — persisted across sessions."

---

### [2:55–3:00] Mobile Responsive

**Action:** Open DevTools → Toggle Device Toolbar → Set to iPhone 14 (390×844). Show the wizard and results page in mobile layout.

**Talking point:**
> "Fully responsive — the wizard and report work on mobile without horizontal scroll."

---
